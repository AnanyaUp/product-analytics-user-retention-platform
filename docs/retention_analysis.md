# Retention Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to measure how effectively the platform retains users after their first interaction.

Retention is one of the most important product analytics metrics because it indicates whether users continue to find value in the platform over time.

---

# Methodology

For each user:

1. First visit date was identified.
2. Subsequent activity dates were tracked.
3. Days since first visit were calculated.
4. Retention rates were computed for Day 1 through Day 30.

---

---

# Key Findings

## Significant Early Drop-Off

The largest decline occurs immediately after user acquisition.

Only 3.89% of users return on the following day.

This means over 96% of users do not return after their first interaction.

---

## Weak Long-Term Retention

Retention falls below 2% within the first week.

By Day 30, virtually no users remain active.

---

## Consistency With Other Metrics

These findings align with previous analyses:

- Stickiness = 4.16%
- View → Purchase Conversion = 1.93%
- Average DAU = 19,685
- MAU = 473,566

All metrics indicate strong acquisition but weak retention.

---

# Business Interpretation

The platform successfully attracts users but struggles to convert them into recurring active users.

This behavior is common in marketplaces where users visit only when they have an immediate purchase need.

---

# Recommendations

## Improve User Re-Engagement

Potential initiatives include:

- Personalized recommendations
- Email marketing campaigns
- Push notifications
- Product watchlists
- Loyalty programs

---

## Increase User Value After First Visit

Focus on creating reasons for users to return by:

- Enhancing discovery experiences
- Providing personalized content
- Improving recommendation systems

---

# Conclusion

Retention is currently the platform's largest challenge.

Improving retention rates would likely have a greater business impact than further increasing acquisition volume.
