# Cohort Analysis

## Objective

The objective of cohort analysis is to understand how user retention varies across different acquisition groups.

Users were grouped into weekly cohorts based on their first activity date.

# Key Findings

## Cohort Retention Declines Rapidly

All cohorts exhibit a sharp decline after the acquisition week.

Retention rates fall below 20% after the first week for every cohort.

---

## Mid-November Re-Engagement Effect

The first cohort shows an unusual pattern:

| Week   | Retention |
| ------ | --------: |
| Week 1 |    11.43% |
| Week 2 |    16.04% |

Week 2 retention exceeds Week 1 retention.

---

## Possible Explanation

This pattern coincides with the large DAU spike observed during mid-November.

Potential causes include:

- Promotional campaigns
- Seasonal sales events
- Marketing initiatives
- Platform-wide discounts

The event appears to have reactivated previously inactive users.

---

## Weak Ongoing Retention

Despite temporary reactivation effects, retention declines steadily across all cohorts.

No cohort demonstrates strong long-term engagement.

---

# Business Interpretation

The platform is capable of generating temporary engagement spikes through external initiatives.

However, these spikes do not translate into sustained user retention.

This suggests that users are motivated primarily by short-term incentives rather than ongoing platform value.

---

# Recommendations

## Replicate Successful Campaigns

Investigate the drivers behind the mid-November engagement spike and determine whether similar initiatives can be repeated.

---

## Improve User Stickiness

Potential initiatives include:

- Personalized product recommendations
- Rewards programs
- Wishlist functionality
- Targeted re-engagement campaigns

---

## Measure Cohort Performance Continuously

Future product experiments should be evaluated using cohort retention metrics rather than acquisition metrics alone.

---

# Conclusion

Cohort analysis confirms that user acquisition is strong but retention remains weak.

The platform's greatest opportunity lies in increasing long-term engagement and repeat usage.
