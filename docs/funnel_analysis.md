# Funnel Analysis

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to understand how users move through the purchasing journey and identify stages where significant drop-offs occur.

The funnel consists of three stages:

1. View Product
2. Add to Cart
3. Purchase

---

# User Funnel

| Stage    | Users   |
| -------- | ------- |
| View     | 451,015 |
| Cart     | 28,864  |
| Purchase | 8,688   |

# Key Findings

## Major Drop-Off Before Cart

The largest user loss occurs between the View and Cart stages.

More than 93% of users who view products do not add them to their cart.

This indicates that users are discovering products but are not progressing to purchase intent.

---

## Stronger Conversion After Cart

Users who add products to their cart convert at a significantly higher rate.

Approximately 30% of cart users complete a purchase.

This suggests that the checkout process is not the primary source of friction.

---

## Revenue Concentration

The platform generated total revenue of 2.66 million from 8,942 purchases.

The average order value is approximately 297.

This indicates a healthy transaction value despite low overall conversion rates.

---

# Business Interpretation

The platform appears effective at attracting traffic but less effective at converting visitors into engaged shoppers.

The primary opportunity for improvement lies in increasing the percentage of users who move from viewing products to adding them to their cart.

---

# Recommendations

## Improve Product Pages

Potential improvements include:

- Better product descriptions
- Higher quality images
- Product comparison features
- Customer reviews and ratings

---

## Improve Product Discovery

Potential initiatives include:

- Personalized recommendations
- Related product suggestions
- Search optimization
- Category navigation improvements

---

## Experiment With Promotions

Potential experiments include:

- Discount offers
- Free shipping thresholds
- Limited-time promotions
- Bundled product offers

---

# Conclusion

The primary business challenge is not checkout conversion but user progression from product discovery to purchase intent.

Future analyses should focus on user retention, cohort behavior, and long-term engagement to understand whether users return after their initial interaction.
